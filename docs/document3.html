The Facebook Dilemma Part 1

What Facebook does with data

The Facebook Dilemma PBS Documentary Summary

The Facebook Dilemma Part 1

The PBS Frontline documentary descriptively reviews how insecure Facebook is for it’s users because of a massive data breach that released the personal information of millions of users on the internet. Moreover, Mark Zuckerberg admits that he had to get people on Facebook to enter their information on his website to gain traffic. “Facebook is an online directory for colleges um I realized that because I didn’t have people’s information I needed to make it interesting enough so that people would wanna use the site and wanna like put their information up.” (Mark Zuckerberg 2:20-2:37) Therefore, Facebook’s initial intent was for people to give their information to Facebook because the website seemed appealing. Moreover, one of Facebook’s most popular feature was NewsFeed which was a system that would show Facebook users news that seemed interesting to them based on their behavior on Facebook which initially seemed desirable. However, the NewsFeed invades someone’s digital privacy because it analyzes their interests on Facebook. For example, NewsFeed analyzes browsing habits, what pages the user is browsing, pages that they are interested in, and many other personal details. Similarly, the Facebook dilemma documentary also states how Facebook was only concerned about massive growth and not about the website’s safety. Tim Sparapani who was Facebook’s Public Policy Director states “We took a very libertarian perspective… we felt some reluctance to interpose our value system on this world wide community that was growing.”(14:19-15:00 Tim Sparapani). Therefore, online safety on Facebook was not guaranteed because they were reluctant to manage content. It is critical for companies like Facebook to prioritize online safety over growth because it would be more difficult later to ban users from Facebook and or file police reports if a person is instigating illegal activity on Facebook if Facebook does not stop the illegal activity immediately. It is Facebook’s obligation to manage content on their website because it can prevent people from instigating illegal activity or illegal speech on Facebook and can deter cyber criminals from using Facebook for illegal reasons.
