Internet Privacy Laws

Internet privacy laws Revealed- how your personal information is protected online

In the present day internet there has to be legal regulations in place to prevent major websites from exploiting the data they receive from users when people browse their websites. For example, in the Thomson Reuters article Internet privacy laws revealed how your personal information is protected online it underlines numerous laws in place to safeguard people’s safety and privacy on the internet. Thomson Reuters states. “The Federal Trade Commission Act (FTC)[1914]– regulates unfair or deceptive commercial practices. The FTC is the primary federal regulator in the privacy area and brings enforcement actions against companies. This includes failing to comply with posted privacy policies and failing to adequately protect personal information.”(Thomson Reuters) Therefore, major companies have to clearly state what they do with customer data before customer data is given to a company to prevent companies from illegally profiteering off of customer information. Moreover, it is crucial that people browse the internet cautiously because they need to be aware that at any moment a company could utilize their data for advertising purposes as discussed earlier with the lookalike advertising strategy. Moreover, it is disadvantageous to allow advertisers to own excessive data about people who browse the internet because for the sake of advertising it is unnecessary to hold precise details about customers such as tracking their browsing habits between websites.
